Two-Stage CMOS amplifier with current mirror and source follower

What I built

  • Architecture: mirror bias → NMOS diff pair with PMOS active-load → NMOS common-source + Miller \(C_c\) → NMOS source follower → inverting feedback.
  • Unity-gain estimate: \(f_t \approx \dfrac{g_{m1}}{2\pi C_c}\).
  • Miller zero: with only \(C_c\) the zero is RHP at \(\omega_z \approx g_{m2}/C_c\). Add \(R_z\) in series with \(C_c\) so \(\omega_z \approx \dfrac{1}{C_c}\!\left(g_{m2}-\dfrac{1}{R_z}\right)\); pick \(R_z\!\approx\!1/g_{m2}\) to push the zero to LHP or \(\infty\).
  • Noise-gain shaping: small \(C_f\) across \(R_f\) flattens the noise-gain at crossover; a handy rule is \(C_f \approx C_{in}\,\dfrac{R_f}{R_{in}}\).
Core small-signal facts

\(g_m \approx \dfrac{2I_D}{V_{ov}}\), \(\; r_o \approx \dfrac{1}{\lambda I_D} \approx \dfrac{V_A}{I_D}\).

Stage-1 output resistance at node \(v_x\): \(R_x \approx r_{o,n} \parallel r_{o,p}\) (include mirror devices if precise).

Stage-1 gain: \(\boxed{A_{v1} \approx g_{m1}\,R_x}\).

Stage-2 gain: \(\boxed{A_{v2} \approx -\,g_{m2}\,(r_{o2}\parallel R_L)}\).

Unity-gain: \(\boxed{f_t \approx \dfrac{g_{m1}}{2\pi C_c}}\).

Miller compensation and the “nulling” resistor

With only \(C_c\), the feed-forward path creates a right-half-plane zero at \(\omega_z \approx g_{m2}/C_c\).

Place \(R_z\) in series with \(C_c\). Then \(Y_c(s)=\dfrac{sC_c}{1+sR_zC_c}\) and the zero shifts to \(\displaystyle \omega_z \approx \frac{1}{C_c}\!\left(g_{m2}-\frac{1}{R_z}\right).\) Choosing \(R_z \approx 1/g_{m2}\) cancels the zero (or moves it to the LHP if \(R_z < 1/g_{m2}\)).

Second pole: \(p_o \approx -\dfrac{1}{(r_{o2}\parallel R_L)\,(C_L + C_o)}\); keep it above the unity crossing for comfortable phase margin.

Closed-loop view (noise-gain & stability)

In inverting mode, closed-loop gain is \(A_v \approx -R_f/R_{in}\). The noise-gain is \(N_0 = 1 + R_f/R_{in}\), so \(f_{BW} \approx \dfrac{\text{GBW}}{N_0}\) with \(\text{GBW}\approx f_t\) for a one-pole open-loop.

Choose \(C_f\) across \(R_f\) to flatten noise-gain near crossover: \(\boxed{C_f \approx C_{in}\,\dfrac{R_f}{R_{in}}}\), where \(C_{in}\) includes gate and stray input capacitances.

KCL cheat-sheet (three-node form)

Let unknowns be the summing node \(v_n\), the internal node \(v_x\), and output \(v_o\). With \(Y_c(s)=\dfrac{sC_c}{1+sR_zC_c}\), \(Y_{in}=1/R_{in}+sC_{in}\), \(Y_f=1/R_f+sC_f\), \(Y_x=1/r_{o1}+sC_{1p}\), \(Y_o=1/R_L+1/r_{o2}+sC_L\):

\(Y_{in}(v_n - v_{in}) + Y_f(v_n - v_o)=0\)

\(-g_{m1}v_n + (Y_x+Y_c)v_x - Y_c v_o = 0\)

\((Y_o+Y_c)v_o + (-Y_c - g_{m2})v_x = 0\)

Solving yields the closed-loop transfer, which reduces to \(-R_f/R_{in}\) at low frequency and exposes how \(C_c, R_z, C_f\) shape the loop.

Build notes that mattered
  • Keep the Miller path \(v_o \leftrightarrow v_x\) very short; long jumpers add parasitic L and C.
  • Decouple \(V_{DD}\) near the second stage; star the mirror, tail source, and output return grounds.
  • If you change \(R_{in}\) for different gains, re-check \(C_f\) so the noise-gain stays tame.
